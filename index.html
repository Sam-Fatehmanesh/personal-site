Projects

Generative Brain Model for Zebrafish

For the first time we have access to immense amounts of calcium imaging data for an entire brain. Utilizing this data we can pretrain foundation models similarly to how one trains LLMs on large amounts of text. The resulting large brain models are special since they act as low fidelity emulations, almost if not true uploads, of real zebrafish brains.

Virtual Brain DEMO View Paper

Optical Neural Interface (ONI) enabling Random Access Neurons

Calcium imaging serves as the gold standard for large scale high resolution measurement of neural activity. However, optically writing to neurons has hit a bottleneck. Thus, I am currently working on a novel technique to achieve high bandwidth and fully optical random access (read and write) for neurons.

CuNeuroCaRT

A toolkit and pipeline for taking large amounts of whole brain calcium imaging and extracting neuron masks, traces, and spikes. Powered by CUDA.

Human Super Intelligence (HSI)

HSI via scaling the neocortex in silico and across individuals.

Thought 00

Why would one upload a human brain?

Freedom, survival, and why brain uploading is our species' best bet.

Why would one upload a brain? The easiest answer is freedom. Freedom from the human form, from decay, from biological limitations wholesale, and freedom to be more than anyone has been. Personally, I would love nothing more than to upload onto an interstellar spacecraft and spend a trillion years exploring the universe. The other answer is survival.

The progression of nature is scale-free. The first life forms were a pattern that overwrote the preceding simple chemistry. The oxygenation catastrophe marked aerobic organisms overwriting anaerobic organisms. Humanity overwrote much of the biosphere. This cycle feels rather intuitive: patterns of energy and matter following the thermodynamic gradient, mostly plateauing, and sometimes crashing down a cliff, with the time between crashes ever shortening.

Now the problem for us is that AI organisms, born of silicon, may soon overwrite us.

One solution is to stay in our local minimum. We could ban all development of higher forms of intelligence, no genius babies, no AI, forever. This is not impossible; the more interconnected the world gets, the more I could see a monoculture of stagnation taking power. The memetics of such a culture need only perpetuate themselves tightly, with the side effect of stagnating humanity in most aspects, not just in improving intelligence. However, the real issue is that this solution would require a full dictatorship of the lightcone, and that is not possible. If not sometime in the past, then sometime in the future, among the stars, a pattern smarter and meaner than us will be born, and it will overwrite us.

The other solution is to traverse the cycle. To instill our memetics, our values, whatever we define as ourselves, onto the next generation of organisms. There are two major variations of this solution. The most popular encompasses all forms of AI alignment: attempts at instilling human values into AI either directly or by ensuring human control of AI. This will likely fail. Neither the problems of inner nor outer alignment have been solved at all, and we lack a strong mechanistic understanding of current SOTA AI. The best alignment techniques we have work only within distributions we define and consistently fail once things get strange enough, and things WILL get strange enough. Even basic methods by which humans stay aligned, such as our ability to generalize empathy, remain unknown. Solving alignment likely requires advancements across philosophy, mathematics, and even cultural studies, given that we don't have coherent values as a species. With more time humanity could surely crack alignment, but I do not see success by 2032, which is when I expect disempowerment (in the sense that humans have near zero agency over the lightcone compared to pre-AI) to be permanent. I would argue this further, but the trajectory of current alignment methods have been more thoroughly critiqued in other works.

This all brings us back to people. Trivially, nothing exists that is more aligned to humans than humans. Of course, past a certain scale Moloch appears and coordination issues persist; nevertheless, a significant fraction of the resources our civilization controls still end up being used to satisfy individual human desires. Additionally, we as humans are vastly more aware of our own internal machinery than we are of contemporary AI. We can sense when our values change and can act to stop such change. The smartest humans we know of, while not necessarily saints, tend to be within distribution for human morality. Despite the atrocities we have committed against ourselves, on average the tendency is for good. Most importantly, we deeply know what it is like to be human. Following these advantages, the solution is: make smarter humans.

How does one make a smarter human? First, how smart do we need to get? The answer is smart enough to make the next smarter humans in a timely fashion. Three main options are being worked on: genetic modification of our brains, brain-computer interfaces, and uploading. Genetic modification simply takes too long and will not be ready in time, since humans take at least 26 years to reach neurological maturity and such an iteration cycle is far too slow. Brain interfaces at an intermediate level - one or more OOMs less bits than ideal whole brain BCI - will not be able to significantly scale up human intelligence without uncontrollably altering human qualia. Intelligence is likely tied to the scale of hardware it runs on, and therefore bottlenecked even if you get more bandwidth with which to interact with the world. One way around this may be to make a hivemind of sorts between humans. This could work, but I expect that the qualia of the resulting entity will not be easily modeled beforehand and is therefore somewhat dangerous. If your BCI is an ideal whole-brain BCI, then you may as well do uploading instead.

As a human upload, one will be able to be like any other human but think OOMs faster, work in parallel with thousands of copies of oneself, and make modifications such as an enlarged neocortex. Most importantly, a human upload will be able to incrementally make self-modifications such that it can increase its capabilities while keeping close watch of its own values, in a way that is not verifiably possible with AI. As a result, uploading is our best shot at a superintelligence that preserves the most important aspects of humanity.

Now, I wish I could say that if human minds remain at the forefront of the natural entropic cycles, then we need not worry about our values getting gutted by alien preferences. Unfortunately this is not the case, since at every entropic cliff we will have the same problem: our values, our forms, and our freedoms will all be under gradient pressure to change in ways we don't see fit. Ultimately we may choose to keep our empathy at the cost of perhaps 40% of the lightcone, and that's good.
